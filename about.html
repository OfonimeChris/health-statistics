Variable Description.

We have Five (5) Variables in our Dataset. PatientID, Age, Gender, BMI and BloodPressure

PatientID: This is an identifier that is unique to each patient or individual in the dataset. It is a numerical value assigned to distinguish between different people.

Age:Age represents the individual's age in years. It's an important factor in health analysis because certain health conditions and risks are frequently linked to specific age groups.

Gender:Gender identifies an individual's biological sex. It is usually classified as "male" or "female." Gender can be used to assess various health risks and conditions because certain diseases may be more prevalent in one gender than the other.

BMI (Body Mass Index): BMI is a measurement based on a person's weight and height. It is calculated by dividing weight in kilograms by height in meters squared. BMI estimates body fat and assists in categorizing individuals as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. It is frequently used to assess the risk of weight-related health conditions such as heart disease, diabetes, and others.

Blood Pressure:Blood pressure is the force exerted by circulating blood on the walls of blood vessels. Blood pressure readings aid in assessing the cardiovascular system's health and indicating potential risks for heart disease, stroke, and other conditions.
